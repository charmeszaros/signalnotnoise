Opening Keynote
Dr Sarah McKay
On the Same Wavelength
Neuroscientist
We have spent five years optimising for async — fewer meetings, more autonomy. So why do so many teams still feel disconnected? Sarah is a neuroscientist who studies what actually builds trust between people — the biological version, not the framework version. When we are genuinely in sync, our heartbeats, brainwaves and hormones start to align. Her keynote draws on two-person neuroscience research to ask: how do you create that synchrony deliberately, even when half your team is on a different Slack timezone?
Spotlight Interview
Gek Peng Tay (GP)
Cleared for Takeoff
Founder, RAiD · Chief Digital Officer, Republic of Singapore Air Force
When the stakes are national security, slow technology is not a backlog issue — it is a vulnerability. GP founded RAiD, Singapore's first military tech startup, and spent 30 years leading change inside one of the highest-stakes environments on the planet. Recognised as a Global Top 100 Innovator in Data and Analytics, GP joins us for a live interview with Aplo CEO Maria Raymond. Come for the story. Leave with the courage to rethink your own transformation.
Talk
Simon Woods
Prompt. Ship. Repeat.
Global Head of UX, Employment Hero
The question at Employment Hero was never if they would use AI — it was how far and how fast. Simon has been running at pace long enough to know what the view looks like from the front. He shares what AI-assisted product design actually looks like inside a team moving at speed: the rhythm, the decisions, and what it takes to stay ahead without losing the craft.
Workshop
Amy Sincock
Lights Up: Presenting Without Panic
Senior Consultant, Aplo
What do performers and great presenters have in common? They both know how to own a room. Amy is an experience strategist and performer of 10+ years — improv comedy and burlesque at Fringe Festivals around the world. This workshop covers managing nerves, crafting your narrative, reading the audience, and recovering when things go off-script. No theory. Just techniques that work when the pressure is on.

Workshop
Ryan & Ant
Build the Vibe
Senior Consultant, Aplo
You do not need to know how to code. You do not even need a laptop. Ryan and Anton build live on screen — but the room is in control. The audience picks what to build, writes the prompts, predicts what happens and diagnoses what breaks. You are not watching a demo. You are running one. Participatory, beginner-friendly, and genuinely surprising. Curiosity is the only prerequisite.
Talk
Pavi De Alwis
Founder's story of a viral corporate startup
Product Engineering Lead, Revity
Most corporate innovation stories end the same way: energy, a compelling pitch, then nothing. Two failed fintech attempts will do that to you — but the third time, something clicked. Pavi has 20 years building at scale across fintech, payments and platform engineering. He takes us inside the third attempt: what changed across strategy, leadership, stakeholder dynamics, and the daily decisions that determine whether a corporate startup finds its feet or flatlines.

Talk
Maria Muir
House of AI and the AI Enabled Enterprise
Technology and AI Strategy Consultant
Your teams are using AI. Your operating model is from 2009. Most organisations are experimenting with new tools but almost none are rethinking how they actually work. Maria works with technology leaders, product teams and executives to close that gap. Her talk is about what happens when you stop treating AI as a technology initiative and start rethinking your operating model around it. AI will not transform your organisation. Changing how you work with it will.
Closing Keynote
Gareth Holebrook
From Intention to Action
Ironman Athlete · Agile Coach · Author
We set the intention. We build the plan. We really mean it this time. So why do so many good commitments fall apart? Gareth is an Ironman athlete, agile coach and author who has spent decades at the intersection of endurance sport and organisational leadership. Most commitments do not fail because people lack motivation — they fail earlier, at the point of decision. His keynote introduces the Ishi Spiral, a practical framework for governing intent. Turns out the Ironman and the boardroom have more in common than you would think.
